Unity 3D ¬∑ Maya
</Back to Life>

Built within 72 hours, this is the game our group (consisted of Allen Weng, Devon Chan, and me) created from scratch for the Ludum Dare 55 Game Jam under the theme "Summoning". This game is partially inspired by puzzle games like "Return of the Obra Dinn", requiring the player to explore their room and bring objects back to life by piecing together dialogues from unknown objects. This is our first attempt at making a 3D game on Unity, and it has required us to learn a lot of stuff in terms of 3D movement, actions, and special effects. On top of that, building an unlockable system has been a fun challenge that, although requires quite a lot of bug fixing, has proved to be a really cool mechanic in showing both the player's progress and the puzzles themselves. We used a linked-list-esque structure at first to link each object with the next associated unlockable object, but due to the number of objects we have designed to be on the scene and the number of unlockables unlocked per time (our game will unlock new objects after three unlocked objects have been correctly matched to their respective lines), we changed the mechanic such that it's more randomized and prevents the players from softlockinng themselves during gameplay.

</Kuso Neko>
Unity 2D ¬∑ Photoshop
</Kuso Neko>

Built within 72 hours, this is the game our group (consisted of Allen Weng, Devon Chan, Nico Xi, and me) created from scratch for the Global Game Jam 2024 under the theme "Make Me Laugh". It is a humorous take on the Japanese otaku culture and also the gameplay style in an infinite runner. The player controls Neko, the cat that broke a figurine of his otaku owner. We managed to balance the game and encourage resource management through a hunger meter, where the player becomes more hungry when he/she moves, uses a skill, or bumps into obstacles, and more full when they eat. On top of that, the player's base speed will be higher the more hungry he/she is and slower when more full. This forces the player to actively manage how much food they should eat instead of mindlessly consuming all the food.\

</Glutton Free>

Built within 72 hours, this is the game our group (consisted of Allen Weng, Devon Chan, and me) created from scratch for the Ludum Dare 54 Game Jam under the theme "Limited Space". In this game, the player controls a softbody bacteria that is trying to survive from an evil scientist who is constantly feeding it gluttonous food. The player's goal is to avoid as much gluttonous food as possible to avoid growing too big and dying. The player can push the bacteria around and dash, both done with the mouse. The player can also grow smaller by eating the floating probiotics to offset the damage done by the food.

Built within 48 hours, this is the game our group (consisted of Allen Weng, Devon Chan, Nico Xi, and me) created from scratch for the GMTK Gam Jam 2023 under the theme "Roles Reversed". We built this game with the concept of reversing the gameplay of a roguelike from both our movement and our design. We made the bold decision to swap the roles of a character and its weapon, such that the weapon uses the character to attack and move on its own. The player can't move as well so the only way to protect itself is to attack and move different movable objects to block the incoming enemies.

</Tap Tap>
ReactJS ¬∑ Firebase ¬∑ Axios ¬∑ Bootstrap ¬∑ Figma
</Tap Tap>

An NFC-based online menu services that allows users to make orders at a restaurant with a single tap on any NFC tags placed on the table. We have complete support of major payment methods, ranging from credit cards to Apple Pay and PayPal, with the help of Stripe. We store all our restaurant and order informations on FirebaseFirestore. Data is transferred through a customly built socket.io connection to enable real-time and low latency communication between our database and the client sessions.

It is still under development and will be published as soon as possible!

</Wandering Pac-Man>

The final group project developed by Yifei Zhou, Leqi Tian, Robin Li, and me for CS 3110 (Data Structures and Functional Programming). We decided to recreate a version of Pac-Man where all the ghosts are blindfolded and wandering around the maze while we control the Pac-Man to eat all the dots. </Pac-Man 3D>

A 3D remake of the classic Pac-Man game with inspiration from Dark Deception. This game features the classic Pac-Man on his quest trying to eat as many gems as possible without being caught by the four ghosts that are controlled by Unity NavMesh Agents with the same algorithm as that of the classical Pac-Man using the Unity 3D's built-in NavMesh Agents. This game also improves on the original approach of input control from only using the arrow keys to control the movement of Pac-Man to being able to control the movement of Pac-Man with both arrow keys and mouse.
